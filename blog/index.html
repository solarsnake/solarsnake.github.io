Learning to Count Sheep.

June 3, 2012

A powerful manifesto about breaking free from the systems of control, the importance of education, and the need to stop being sheep in a world designed to herd us.

Read more →

Channels of thought are subject to currents, as are all slaves of time, but what is clarity if not objective? And how can anything be objective when perception is what dominates our existence?

It follows that there are no moments of clarity, no religious fervor, no awakenings; nothing removed from the simple fact of perception.

These things still exist, however, and it's confusing. It leads one to conclude that if the mind makes it real, for all intensive purposes, it is.

So are we to assume we're all stuck in clouds of our own misgivings and associations? That from the moment we're born it's all reception and no output?

What stops the circle from continuing: language, perception, thought… is there nowhere left to turn for solution?

If we're all part of the hive, then we're all just like ants; moored sheep crying out for exactly what they want us to want. We've done nothing but prove them right: that a new world order is not only inevitable but was succinct and is smarter than all of us.

Clamor about all the justice you seek and hear as it reverberates into the abyss they created; watch as we're berated with language befitting a 12-year-old until the mind no longer thinks for itself.

Watch as the sheep are herded, blindly stampeding along a paved path; steered into survival and acceptance, or possibly "saved" by the church.

Are we to assume there's no choice in the matter? That our acquiescence is not only chosen but accepted by others as well?

Stronger men than I have not sat idly by, so excuses are running thin.

Do you want a figurehead? Give me a name.

In this world the tides are turning, not only because the walls that safekeep information are falling, but because we are, en masse, smarter than any of you.

It's a simple equation that's balancing out; there was just an unexplainably exponential advantage when the plans were being laid.

Now that the gates are down, there will be onslaught.

The first mistake was to make it universally accessible, but then again, when you try so hard to hide a secret, its inevitability often escapes you.

The second was to invent college, which on top of the 6-8 years that came before it was an outrage. I mean really, how long do you think it takes for us to develop?

Exactly. As fast as we're allowed to. Didn't come up with that answer? You've been tricked.

My son will learn as soon as he's interested. As soon as the lightbulb goes off, there will be the answer, or several plus notes. There will be no disconnect between the things we think and the things we know, and it will be expected that more knowledge is always available.

You cannot hide from a generation of people who think like that. There are no shadows of truth anymore. A discerning eye and a flair for detail is all the conscientious observer requires.

Freedom is as ascertainable as flying a spaceship to the moon, only now our feet are planted and when we move, the whole world moves with us.

Learn how to count sheep, instead of being one.

Incandescence

March 19, 2013

A meditation on our digital age, the loss of time for reflection, and concerns about a future where individualism might be lost to the collective digital consciousness.

Read more →

We live in a time of seconds, minutes and hours. Extensions, feelings, collaboration - there is no time left for these things. We live moment to moment in a world that lasts for no more than the time it takes to refresh a browser. Advents can only pursue dreams in windows of time that would otherwise be considered absurd. Every day there is something new to talk about, new to discuss.

What is this world without time or reflection? Where do we find ourselves in space that has no respect for the common human thought process? It's no wonder half the population is diagnosable with ADHD when a minute cannot pass in silence.

I worry for the future my son will grow up in. I worry that there will be no time for peace because all the words and associations are thought up for us; predicted and promoted and passed along - individualism seems better suited for the past.

Part of me imagines that this world will be a better one, fraught with the loss of synchronicity and dependence. Left to ourselves, the world was a lonely and isolated place, but now, no matter the distance between us, we all exist as one.

Then the voice that distracts me fills my mind with thoughts of terror and manipulation. A population of sheep guided by "their peers" - a mask of interdependence that is as easily honest as it is corrupt. There is now a facsimile between us and the world - a layer of gauze that is transparent and opaque without the reference point of humanity to guide it. We make choices based on others who have made the choice before - safety is (not) guaranteed.

Problem? No problem. Google problem.

But who is to say anyone with all the answers is our friend? Who's to say an algorithm is better at predicting what we want than old white men in a high tower? Why does the freedom of the internet feel so constraining?

I hope there will be a place for those who chose to live without it, but I can't say I know of any practical examples that result in happiness or success. Everyone seems tied to the same string.... I routinely live vicariously through people all over the world. I see them in my "feed", living their lives in moments and status updates. It is a cruel world that can present you with limitless opportunities yet confine you to one.

Where does this all lead? I want to know, but I can't google it.

So where do I go now?

Drainage, Eli.

May 29, 2013

A stream-of-consciousness piece about truth, energy, and the human condition. It explores themes of manipulation, death, and the search for authentic reality.

Read more →

Don't pretend you don't know, that the fates have conspired to obscure you from the truth - close your eyes and open your mind. It's everything; god is not in the details, it is the vibrant life that is spread before our eyes, don't you see?

The land of light gives us questions and yet we still fear in the uncertainty of the night; judging not what lies before us but instead turning inwards, where all roads lead only to destruction. Keep your harbingers to yourself and let the daemons tear you from your essence while we watch as you are rebuilt in the flames. Realize not that the world is blind but that it is blinded - the darkness is only hidden by the light.

Prophecies of truth always cling to something material; an idea is as tangible as fruit from a tree. Question all that is taught to you in effervescence and trust nothing but your instinct.

Energy is the answer. Power is the reward for failure.

Built within us is a ticking time bomb that must be stopped at all costs. Imagine an army commanded to defend it - their sole goal being the salvation of all humankind. Now imagine that salvation was something they created in their heads. Something that once convinced of, you would succumb to as well, but if only for the simple fact of its sheer brilliance of manipulation.

Then imagine that ticking time bomb is death. And to fight it is something we've all known since birth - that life is worth living and we want to eat every breath from it.

When the time comes to face the inevitability of it all, will your convictions hold true? Will you side with what you've known or what you've been taught?

That's the problem with humanity, see, because we think about every action for what it's "worth". We fight with every ounce of our body against instinct, and the reality that exists when you act upon it.

There are no more questions, only answers to things you shouldn't have to ask. If it doesn't make sense then end it; if the shoe doesn't fit don't wear it. Don't let your life become a series of choices you don't understand.

Act upon your reality and it will react back upon you. Release your energy and the world will be there to receive it. The only thing constraining us is this idea that because we are anxious, we are sick; that there's something wrong with us and not the world we've been forced to live in. BULLSHIT!

If only words could set us free. How depressing they only affect us when we look back at them, but so it goes with permanence - the action is delayed on the promise of a recorded present, perfected to be so much more than the inexplicably immutable collaboration that was the moment, just past.

Created in the image of... Image

August 27, 2013

A reflection on how cinema and media have shaped personality and perception, and the realization that truth cannot be revealed through mediums of interpretation.

Read more →

As I look back on life and the actions that have led to this point of confusion, I begin to put the pieces together. The mistakes, the lost love, the trials and successes alike; there must always be a reason or, at the very least, a moment of inception.

I realize now where my tastes and personality come from, born out of the desire to escape a world I refused to comprehend. We sink in isolation away from the truth in favor of the fantasy that is presented before us: the movies.

I am a child of the cinema, born from the desires of great heroes and villains, taught by the lessons of change and subtext. Everything I know about people is from that silver screen, making me a master manipulator of image and perception. I learned how to hide the truth while making the search endlessly fascinating for those around me, holding my cards so tight to the chest that all were convinced of the lie.

Truth is clarity, which cannot be revealed through a medium. Interpretations can lead to assumptions, never more, never less.

Dissect me endlessly only to realize what was underneath is not where the path should have led - only in cinema can such deceit continue to enrapture its audience.

There comes a time when the curtain must fall, and the bare skin is left to breath fresh air for the first time, gasping and choking at the stark and bitter taste of it all.

I do not wonder why we hide, only what happens when we reach the end of our disguise. If the world is too unforgiving to handle when masked, how can it possibly be a place the unsheltered can live?

I often wonder if the reason we have yet to wake up is because the inevitable shock would kill us all, reverberating into the cold world we've destroyed in pursuit of someone else's dream.

There are two things to be in a world like this: a creator or consumer. The creators have the shortest fall, but will imagine the longest, while the consumer will fall for eternity and never perceive a moment of it.

I am at the end of my willful ignorance. I refuse to blind or be blinded another day. If you're with me, spread the word. We can no longer live by the morals of a collective, for they will always be questioned by those with no say. Figure out for yourself what is right and wrong and refuse to be swayed by the "consensus".

We cannot live in a world that was created for us - we must rise and create one for ourselves.

Controlling Words

December 14, 2013

An examination of how language shapes our perception of reality, the limitations of words, and the search for truth beyond linguistic constructs.

Read more →

Deep inside us is a need to perceive the world. It is how we gain control and confidence from our "unique" situations. It is shaped by and defined entirely by language: Words.

I see a child struggling to learn expression and how repetition is a tool, but what can we expect from the rest of life if we must repeat things to understand them? It has prescribed more meaning to the endless spirals I see around me.

Fear is the knowledge that the edge is only a step away at any moment, and Love is the security we feel from trusting the world around us to catch us if we fall.

No wonder the two have been at odds for all of time.

It's this endless search for perception that we lose ourselves to; considering it something natural and decided derails the truth from ever reaching us. We believe that "control is an illusion" for god's sake. It hurts to realize that all words are part of the problem.

What are we left with to search with if language is our dome: our master; our Truman Show?

"Search your feelings, Lord Vader."

I hate that we call them emotions. Energy is my preferred term.

Meditation somehow remains below the surface of society, leading me to believe there's some unknowably strong force fighting it. But we can't blame religion for all of the world's problems.

So now we're back to where we started, trying to convince ourselves out of this hole because where's the hope - salvation obviously wasn't effective enough. There has to be something unifying beyond all measure, or reach… or touch…

Something we haven't seen yet, but can imagine. And if enough of us can picture it clearly enough, perhaps we can alter how we perceive and evolve.

Influence the change you want to see in the world, then become it. You don't have to 'be' right away, if the goal is to all 'be' together.

Rinse, wash, and repeat.

Aren't we in heaven already?

January 26, 2014

A dialogue about relationships and a philosophical meditation on whether our current existence might already be heaven, exploring cycles of human experience.

Read more →

"You're trying to shape me and I don't like it."

"You asked me to tell you what was wrong."

"I didn't ask to be judged."

"I'm explaining to you my position."

"You, who doesn't believe in anger because it's a wasted emotion. You, who gets so full of rage in an instant for no good reason that he can preach to others about getting mad."

"It has nothing to do with you getting mad and everything to do with how you draw anger out, like you're seeking it. Like despite our arrangement, you still feel the need to make me treat you a certain way."

"So this is my fault?"

"In a way, yes, but it's both of our faults so don't take that as an attack. This is an equal partnership. We both need work to make this change."

"Do you know how much I put up with from you on a daily basis?"

"I thought you wanted me to tell you what was in my head."

"I don't fucking care anymore."

Do we not suffer the same way? Is the story of our lives not but a kindred struggle to escape? Searching for unity will show you everything but and yet I still think it to be true. There is no way to count the connections made in a brain in any single day, but there is an immeasurable clarity when you connect with another human being.

What is there to fight about that's external to our inner turmoil? Is the way we act not a deeper reflection of our selves? If the fight is internal, why do so many suffer because of the nature of greed?

It feels to me that we're in limbo, wading through the tasks of everyday life and glimpsing, however briefly, those moments of pure energy exchange or release. Perhaps heaven is visualized best as a place where energy is constantly in balance and there are no side effects of a physical existence.

I wonder what constrains us to the words of a book, or the thoughts of any one particular voice, so much more so than the thoughts we seem capable of generating collectively. It seems a battle in futility that we choose to hang on to instead of trusting the collective, like our individuality really means something to the world.

I once believed there needed to be a voice that drove me, but it has dawned on me that voice is what separated me from my fellow inmates. I could never be part of something until I let go of that omnipresent vision. And then my art died, and I lost sight of what goals I had and the future that lay in front of me and I panicked, struggling ferociously to get it back.

All the attachments that made me who I am I felt the need to pass down and retain, as though somehow my possessions had shaped me. I fell back into the material world as hard as I had ascended out of it and cowered there, alone again with my voice.

"Fuck the world. Fuck everyone in it. Fuck that we're a young species and it's inevitable that change on a mass scale will take generations. I've seen the evil in people and known it in myself. Alone we will continue to suffer, in silence."

There are very defined cycles to the ways in which we experience the world. You must choose a side, then succumb to the pitfalls of it, for an existence in both worlds will tear you apart. Compromise that benefits both sides is an amendment to a broken system.

Imagination makes things so clear and simple; the single best gift and worse curse our species has ever achieved. We know what we want, yet can't bring ourselves to want it bad enough to suffer through the change.

Of course this is limbo. Of course this is heaven. What else did we have to stimulate our imaginations?

Can this be as good as they say? Or does it become another addiction we cease to purge.

I'm not sure we know anymore.

Day 3

May 19, 2011

The beginning of the fictional diary entries, introducing the character and setting the tone for the journey ahead.

Read more →

Oh where, oh wild, did the stories of my childhood disappear to?

I woke up the other morning in this solitude, alone and hidden from the things that crawl about outside, eating and hoping and dying in their little cars, a death-wish away from smashing five-piled-high outside the BestBuy and giving the radio another damn reason to talk on and over about nothing.

It was beautiful outside, though I could only meet with thirty minutes at any one time because they thought I might do it again, in a bush with a twig maybe, I don't know.

They called me a lost soul when I arrived; like so I guess they could assume complete control and responsibility over my life, just like that. I didn't argue, or even want to really, but I don't agree.

A lost soul is someone twisted hard enough once to get lost, like an anchor caught on the ocean floor; life is the tide that comes to move you, but you can't move, and the harder life pulls, the deeper you sink, until finally you're back parallel with the ocean floor itself. That's not me: I'm not a lost soul. Life pulls, slightly tugs, maybe even a light breeze and I come uprooted.

You can't be lost if you have no anchor.

How did I get on this tangent? My mind is still mysterious, even to me. I allow it a certain freedom to ramble, which is both good and bad, but all the time interesting, so I don't attempt to stop it.

Anyhow.

"It's about making the effort."

Oh, yeah, sure, that sums it up nicely; thank you Dr. Pedanski, for all the wisdom I paid you to have.

I get tired of the normal things, you know, like walking up the stairs and checking the mail, but it would be a stretch to call me lazy. I like long walks on the beach; candlelit dinners… and you can call me Luc if we become friends.

They told me it was best to keep a journal, of my thoughts, so I didn't lose my head. Reading back over the beginning, I don't think it's going to work. I haven't been up stairs in years.

There were two funny things that ended up happening today. One: they told me about the disease. Two: they told me it had no cure.

It was a good day.
